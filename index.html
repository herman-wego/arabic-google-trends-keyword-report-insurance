US-Israel military strikes on Iran (28 Feb 2026) caused 23,000+ flight cancellations, airspace closures across the Gulf. Arabic speakers worldwide are searching for insurance coverage, refund rights, and rebooking help. Emirates and Qatar Airways suspended operations.
War exclusions exposed in travel insurance policies
Standard travel insurance policies broadly exclude war-related claims. Arabic media is running stories about the "insurance gap" — travelers stranded without coverage. CFAR (Cancel for Any Reason) demand surged 27% since early March.

Health insurance is mandatory for Saudi tourist visa holders. Rising demand from Egyptians and other Arab nationals applying for Saudi e-visas is driving "تأمين السفر" searches, particularly involving Al Rajhi Bank and Tawuniya Insurance products.
Schengen visa insurance demand remains elevated
The EU's new e-Schengen visa system rollout (2025–2026) and ETIAS launch are keeping Schengen travel insurance queries high among Arabic speakers applying from Gulf states, Egypt, Jordan, and the diaspora.

The flagship travel trade show in Dubai was pushed back due to regional instability, reflecting broader travel uncertainty in the MENA region that is fueling insurance-related searches.

Executive Summary

The Iran conflict has created an unprecedented spike in Arabic travel insurance search demand. The dominant theme is crisis-driven: Arabic speakers are searching for war exclusion information, CFAR policies, and how to get refunds for cancelled flights. Google Trends data shows breakout-level interest for Al Rajhi Bank, Spain (as an alternative destination), Egyptians (as a demographic searching for insurance), and the Schengen area — all in the context of "تأمين السفر" (travel insurance). The biggest content opportunity is an authoritative Arabic guide explaining what travel insurance does and does not cover during the current conflict, combined with practical advice on CFAR policies and mandatory insurance requirements for popular visa destinations. Wego should prioritize Arabic crisis-insurance content immediately, while also building evergreen Arabic guides for Schengen and Saudi visa insurance.
